I am a formal semanticist interested in the interaction between semantics and its syntactic and pragmatic interfaces. I am postdoc at Heinrich Heine Universität in Düsseldorf, in the Department of Linguistics.

Broadly speaking, my research focuses on (i) how semantic composition is constrained by the syntactic structure of sentences, and (ii) how the output of the semantic computation is affected by the context of utterance. In the past I have mainly explored these questions by looking at quantification and plurality related phenomena. Topics that I have worked on include numerals, comparatives, superlatives and other degree expressions, genericity and kind predication, focus association and ignorance implicatures.

Currently I have ongoing collaborations with Hana Filip on the semantics of generic statements, with Ethan Poole and Brian Dillon on NPI licensing from an experimental perspective, with Stefan Keine and Ethan Poole on certain aspects of the semantics of tough-constructions, and with Athulya Aravind on the acquisition of weak implicatures.
